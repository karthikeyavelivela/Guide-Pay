Guide-Pay: Parametric Insurance, Automated End-to-End
Worker pays ₹24–56/week. When a qualifying disruption event is verified, payout is issued within 4 hours — no claim, no document, no call centre.
Parametric model: payout is triggered by a verified external event, not by the worker's individual loss. Eliminates moral hazard and adverse selection in one design decision.
3 trigger types: IMD flood alert (state-issued), platform app outage (Downdetector + status API), and government curfew / Section 144 (admin API verification).
Activity verification: checks worker's last_order_timestamp before paying. Worker must have been active in the 2 hours before the event — eliminates cold-start claims.
Premium: ₹24–56/week based on zone flood risk × worker risk score. Coverage cap: ₹600/week. Target loss ratio: 65%.
Firebase OTP onboarding — no documents required at sign-up. Worker is active within 3 minutes of first interaction.
PAYOUT FLOW — TARGET <4 HOURS
1
Trigger event detected (IMD / Downdetector / admin API)
2
Multi-worker correlation confirms event (15+ workers, same zone)
3
Activity gate: last_order_timestamp < 2 hrs → eligible
4

Razorpay UPI credit → worker notified via Firebase push

SPEAKER NOTE
The activity gate is what separates us from a blunt area-based payout. We're not paying everyone in Kondapur when it floods — we're paying the workers who were actively on shift when it became impossible to work. That's the precision underwriters need to maintain a viable loss ratio.

The 3 Triggers: Verifiable, Causal, Bounded
Each trigger passes one test: does the event physically prevent delivery work? If yes, it qualifies. AQI excluded on engineering grounds — platforms don't halt during pollution.

Source: IMD SACHET RSS feed

Platform App Outage
Source: Downdetector + official status API
75% payout cap
Dual-source verification: Downdetector spike + platform's own status page. 75% cap reflects partial income loss — worker may multi-home across platforms.
DIRECT CAUSATION
🚫
Curfew / Section 144
Source: Govt admin API + manual verification
100% payout cap
Legal prohibition on movement in a geo-zone. Verified via district admin API and cross-checked against news API. Hard causal link: no movement, no delivery.
DIRECT CAUSATION
DELIBERATE EXCLUSION — AQI / AIR QUALITY
AQI events fail the causation test. Swiggy, Zepto, and Blinkit do not halt platform operations during pollution events — high-AQI days show normal or elevated order volumes (homebound cooking substitution). Insuring AQI would create a moral hazard: workers incentivised to reduce shifts during poor air regardless of platform demand. Excluding AQI is an actuarial decision, not a limitation. Our competitor Team Code Alchemists includes it — this breaks their causation chain.
SPEAKER NOTE
The AQI exclusion is a deliberate design choice that strengthens our actuarial model. Every trigger we include must demonstrably halt the worker's earning capacity — not merely reduce it subjectively. That rigour is what makes parametric underwriting defensible to a reinsurer.

5 AI Modules: From Prediction to Fraud Prevention
Each module addresses a specific failure mode of parametric insurance — over-payout, fraud, pricing inaccuracy, or operational blindness.
01
Predictive Disruption Engine
XGBoost — 24h ahead flood probability per zone
Inputs: IMD 24h forecast, zone elevation DEM, historical flood events (2015–2024), monsoon index. Outputs a 0–1 probability score per city zone. Enables proactive worker notification and dynamic premium adjustment before an event.
02
Platform Activity Verification
Timestamp logic — last_order_timestamp gating
<2 hrs: full payout eligible. 2–6 hrs: reduced payout. >6 hrs: flagged for manual review. Prevents payout to workers who were already inactive before the disruption event — the most common parametric abuse vector.
03
Real-Time Disruption Heat Map
Interactive India map — admin dashboard
Live payout exposure by city and zone. Color-coded alert levels. Total liability exposure per active event. Enables operations team to monitor, cap, and respond to emerging events before payout batch runs.
04
Worker Risk Score
Behavior-based pricing — 0.0 to 1.0 score
Signals: claim frequency, zone activity consistency, platform tenure, multi-platform activity. Low risk (score <0.4): 0.85× premium multiplier. High risk (score >0.7): 1.15× premium multiplier. Actuarially fair and incentive-compatible.
05
Multi-Worker Event Correlation
Isolation Forest (scikit-learn) — anomaly detection
15+ workers reporting inactivity in the same zone within the same time window = confirmed event, auto-approve. 1 worker = anomaly = manual review. Isolation Forest flags single-worker outliers that don't match zone-level patterns — primary fraud detection layer.

Premium Model & Actuarial Logic
A risk-adjusted, behavior-sensitive premium that prices fairly at ₹24–56/week while maintaining a 65% loss ratio target — the standard benchmark for sustainable micro-insurance.
Weekly Premium =
₹35 × Zone Risk (0.8–1.4)
× Worker Risk Score (0.85–1.15)
Lowest premium: Low-risk worker, low-risk zone (e.g. Kondapur, Hyderabad) → ₹24/week
Highest premium: High-risk worker, flood-prone zone (e.g. Dharavi, Mumbai) → ₹56/week
Coverage cap: ₹600/week — calibrated at 33% of median weekly gig income (₹1,800/week assuming ₹7,200/month median).
Target loss ratio: 65% — industry standard for micro-insurance products. Leaves 35% for operations, reinsurance, and reserves at scale.
ZONE RISK MULTIPLIER LOGIC
Derived from IMD historical flood frequency × zone elevation data from SRTM DEM. Zone 0.8 = <2 flood events/decade, elevation >100m. Zone 1.4 = >8 flood events/decade, low-lying coastal/riverine.
ADVERSE SELECTION DEFENCE
Activity verification gate prevents workers from enrolling only during pre-flood windows. 30-day minimum enrollment before first claim eligibility. Zone registration locked at sign-up — cannot change during active alert.
UNIT ECONOMICS AT 210K WORKERS (YEAR 1)
Avg premium ₹35/week × 52 weeks × 210K workers = ₹38.2 crore ARR. At 65% loss ratio, claims pool = ₹24.8 crore. Operating cost target ≤18% = ₹6.9 crore. Reinsurance buffer: 17%.
SPEAKER NOTE
The 65% loss ratio is conservative for parametric products — the absence of individual claims processing eliminates the 15–20% loss adjustment expense typical in indemnity. Our actual operational loss ratio target is likely achievable at 60% as the claims database matures.
