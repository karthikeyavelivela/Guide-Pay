---

## Why NOT individual platform workers

A worker exclusive to one platform has more income risk from that platform's policies than from external disruptions. Multi-platform workers (2–3 platforms) represent the highest-value segment because:
- External disruptions affect ALL their platforms simultaneously
- They have the most to lose per disruption event
- They already demonstrate financial literacy (managing multiple apps)
- Higher average income = higher coverage cap willingness

## Willingness to Pay Evidence

| Source | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| Dvara Research (2023) | 68% of gig workers willing to pay ₹50–100/month for income protection |
| ILO Gig Economy Report (2022) | 74% of delivery workers cite weather as primary income risk |
| NASSCOM GigWork Study | Average monsoon income loss: ₹2,800/worker/month |

At ₹44/week = ₹176/month — this is within the confirmed willingness-to-pay range for the Low Risk tier.

## Key Insight

Every other insurance product for gig workers is reactive — the worker must file a claim, upload documents, wait for approval.

Guide-Pay's entire value proposition is zero friction. The worker does nothing. This is not a feature — it is the product.
